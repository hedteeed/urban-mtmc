The exact build roadmap — first this, by doing that

Every phase: what we build, the exact doing, what data feeds it, and the test that says "done." Split between YOU (physical world: cameras, walking, clicking, permissions) and ME (all code). The target: hand the system an authorized camera feed → it learns the floor plan from the footage, tracks everyone on one live dashboard, and labels enrolled people (you, the client, their son, a named person of interest) by name in real time. Phases 0–2 are shipped and verified live — this doc reflects the repo as it stands, not a fantasy plan.

PHASE 0

Simulator + dashboard

Shipped ✓

What we built: a fake world so every later phase has a test bench. Two-floor house plan, ~12 simulated people walking a waypoint graph, 6 virtual cameras that "observe" with realistic noise, dropout, and tracker-style per-camera IDs — streaming over WebSocket into a live two-panel floor-plan dashboard.

How it was done: froze the event schema first (5 written invariants — the contract every future real camera must also speak), then three parallel builds against it, then an adversarial review that caught a runtime-only WebSocket blocker the tests missed.

DataNone — synthetic by construction (the mantis synth.py lesson)
Done testDots glide in cones; ground-truth toggle shows continuous paths crossing the blind gap ✓ passed live
Keeps givingThe fusion engine (P6) will be tuned against this sim first — ground truth is free here, priceless later
PHASE 1

First real camera

Shipped ✓

What: your Mac webcam becomes live0 — a real detector's output flowing through the exact same event stream and dashboard as the sim.

  1. ME — fetch COCO-pretrained YOLOX-S as ONNX (Apache-2.0, sha256-pinned, provenance printed). No training: 118k images already taught it what a person looks like.
  2. ME — port the 5-step inference (letterbox 640 → forward → obj×class → un-letterbox → NMS 0.7, person class only) to ONNX Runtime, numpy only.
  3. ME — camera worker thread: grab frames, detect at ~5 fps, emit contract Observations, keep annotated JPEG for a live video tile in the dashboard.
  4. YOU — click Allow on the macOS camera permission, then walk in front of the laptop.
DataPretrained weights only (COCO). Nothing recorded, nothing labeled.
Done testYou appear in the camera tile with a box + confidence following you at ~5 fps
Honest gapNo tracker yet → new ID every frame. No calibration → no dot on the plan. Both deliberate; next two phases.
PHASE 2

Real tracking — stable IDs

Shipped ✓

What: ByteTrack (MIT implementation) between detector and event stream, so one human = one persistent track_id.

  1. ME — Kalman filter per track (predict-then-correct, the Vol 3 §04 math), Hungarian assignment, ByteTrack's two-pass trick: match confident detections first, then rescue occluded people from the low-confidence leftovers.
  2. ME — lower the detector feed threshold to ~0.1 (round 2 fuel), expose track_thresh / match_thresh / track_buffer as flags.
  3. YOU — the tuning loop: walk behind a chair/door frame, watch whether your ID survives. We adjust track_buffer to your room's occlusions together.
DataNone. Trackers are algorithms, not models — tuned live, not trained.
Done testPillar test: walk behind furniture, emerge, same ID ≥ 8/10 times. Sidebar tracklet count ≈ people count, not 5×/second inflation.
PHASE 3

Calibration — dot on YOUR floor plan

Next up

What: the homography (Vol 3 §07's one matrix multiply) that turns your feet-pixel into a floor-plan point. First phase where the real camera puts a dot on the map.

  1. YOU — tape measure: sketch your room to scale (I turn it into a plan.json floor). Mark 8+ floor points you can see on camera (tile corners, furniture feet) and measure their positions.
  2. ME — a click-to-calibrate UI: camera frame beside floor plan, click each point in both, cv2.findHomography(RANSAC), reprojection error shown live (target <0.3 m). Saved per camera. The mantis CalibrationPanel idea, rebuilt for homographies.
  3. ME — project each track's box bottom-center (the feet) through the matrix → floor_xy stops being null → dashboard dots appear, real ones.
  4. YOU — verification walk: stand on 4 known spots, we check the dot lands within ~0.5 m of each.
Data8+ clicked point-pairs per camera. One-time, ~15 min per camera.
Done testYou walk your room; your dot walks the plan, ≤ ~0.5 m error at the known spots
PHASE 4

Second camera + overlap fusion

Soon

What: a second viewpoint (old phone running an RTSP app, ~free) with its own calibration; where views overlap, two dots for one person become one — by geometry alone, no appearance needed.

  1. YOU — install an RTSP camera app on any old phone, mount it (shelf + tape is fine) seeing part of camera 1's area. Both devices on NTP (I script it).
  2. ME — multi-source server (N camera workers), per-camera calibration, then geometric fusion: dots from different cameras within a tolerance radius (~0.5 m) on the plan merge into one displayed person.
  3. YOU — stand in the overlap zone during tuning: too-tight radius shows you doubled, too-loose merges you with a second person standing close. We find your building's number.
DataSecond camera's 8+ calibration clicks; a few two-person walk tests
Done testOne person in the overlap = one dot; two people close together = two dots
PHASE 4A

Floor plan from the feed alone

New · the "walks in and learns the building" phase

What: point the system at an authorized camera system it has never seen and have it learn the geometry itself — no tape measure, no clicking. This is the "hand it to a client, it figures out their space" capability.

The honest decomposition — three sub-problems, very different difficulty:

  1. ME — Walkable map from footfall (fully achievable, killer demo): accumulate every tracked person's foot-path over time; where people walk becomes floor, walls emerge as the voids nobody crosses. The map draws itself — corridors light up as rivers of traffic. Pure statistics, zero training.
  2. ME — Auto-calibration from people (achievable, published technique): humans are the ruler. Average adult height ≈1.7 m; watching heads-and-feet across many walks recovers each camera's tilt and metres-per-pixel automatically, replacing P3's clicked points as footage accumulates.
  3. ME — Camera topology (already P6): which camera connects to which, and transit times, learned from handoff co-occurrence statistics.

The honest ceiling: this produces an accurate walkable map + real-world positions — "which corridor, which room-mouth" — not an architect's blueprint of walls and doors. True wall reconstruction from fixed cameras alone is unsolved without depth sensors; we don't promise it. P3's manual calibration stays as the ground-truth yardstick this auto system is graded against.

DataNo training. Per-site statistics from the client's own feed (hours of footfall). The simulator generates infinite synthetic layouts to validate the algorithm for free.
Done testOn a fresh camera set with no manual input, the learned walkable map + positions land within ~1 m of the P3 hand-calibrated truth
Key insightLayout is estimated per site from statistics, never learned into model weights — so no "dataset of many buildings" is needed; the detector/Re-ID are layout-agnostic
PHASE 5

Re-ID — the appearance fingerprint

Later

What: OSNet embedder (via torchreid, MIT) turning each person-crop into a 512-number signature; per-track running template (EMA). First test is single-camera: leave the room, come back, get your old ID back instead of a new one.

  1. ME — pretrained OSNet on the crop stream, quality gates (min height, edge crops rejected), EMA template per tracklet, cosine matching of new tracks against recently-dead ones.
  2. YOU — re-entry trials: exit frame 30s, return. Also the hard case: you + someone in similar clothes, swap test.
  3. ME — threshold sweep tooling: we plot match-rate vs mistake-rate from your trials and pick the operating point (the fragmentation-vs-collision dial from Vol 2 §05 — now it's YOUR dial on YOUR data).
DataPretrained Re-ID weights (research-only — fine for prototype, flagged for product). Your re-entry trial recordings for threshold tuning.
Done testRe-acquisition: leave + return = old ID ≥ 8/10; look-alike test documented honestly
PHASE 5A

Named people — the enrolled gallery

New · authorized deployments only

What: turn an anonymous dot into a named dot. You give the system a photo of a known person — yourself, the client, their son, a specified person of interest — and when a camera gets a clear look at that face, the dot is labelled with their name and stays labelled as they move through the building.

  1. ME — face detector (finds faces in frame) → face embedder (ArcFace-family → a 512-number face signature) → cosine match against an enrolled gallery you build (5–10 photos per known person). No training — enrolment only. Event gains an identity field; dashboard shows the name on the dot + a "known person seen" notification.
  2. ME — identity rides the body-track: the face only has to match ONCE from a good angle; that name then locks to the person's tracklet (Re-ID) and follows their dot through corridors where the face is unreadable. First good glimpse → named for their whole visit.
  3. YOU — enrol the gallery (drop in photos), then walk past a camera; tune the match threshold on your own trials (too strict misses the client, too loose mislabels a stranger — the same fragmentation-vs-collision dial, higher stakes).

The honest ceiling — state this to anyone: face recognition needs ~50+ pixels of near-frontal face. It works at door / eye-level cameras where people look toward the lens ("is that the client at the door?" — reliable). It frequently cannot fire on ceiling CCTV (tiny, tilted, back-of-head). So the real promise is: labelled on the first good look, carried by body-tracking after — not "every face from every angle instantly." That's physics, not a bug, and we design around it.

DataNo training — enrolment gallery only (5–10 photos per known person, provided with consent). Best open face models (InsightFace) are non-commercial → prototype-flagged in provenance like everything else.
Done testEnrolled person walks past a good-angle camera → correctly named within a second; label persists on their dot across cameras; stranger stays unlabelled
GuardrailAuthorized private deployments only, self-enrolled gallery of known people with consent. The public/mall configuration runs anonymous-Re-ID with faces OFF (EU AI Act line). No matching against external/scraped databases, ever — enforced in config, not just policy
PHASE 6

Cross-camera engine — the product core

The hard one

What: the piece nothing on GitHub gives you: tracklets from all cameras clustered into global IDs under physics constraints. One human = one dot, across blind gaps.

  1. YOU — stopwatch afternoon: walk every camera-to-camera route ~5 times; I record transit-time distributions (the topology graph — highest-leverage hour in the whole project).
  2. ME — the engine: sliding 30–60 s window, hard gates first (impossible transits killed regardless of appearance), fused edge scores (appearance ~50% / space-time ~35% / motion ~15%), agglomerative clustering with cannot-links, global ID gallery with expiry. Tuned against the P0 simulator first — free ground truth — then on real walks.
  3. YOU — the loop walk: cam1 room → hallway blind gap → cam2 room, 10 times. Also the adversarial version with a similarly-dressed second person.
DataTransit-time walks; ~10 labeled loop-walk recordings (I build the tiny labeling tool)
Done testLoop walk keeps ONE global ID ≥ 8/10 — this is the demo that wins hackathons and pilots
PHASE 7

Training round A — YOUR detector

First real training

What: until here, zero training — all pretrained + algorithms + geometry. Now we fine-tune YOLOX-S on your actual cameras, because pretrained stumbles on your exact angles/lighting.

  1. YOU — record per camera: one quiet clip, one busy clip, hard cases (occlusions, sitting). ~15 min each.
  2. ME — CVAT labeling setup; YOU label ~1 evening per camera (boxes every 10th frame, full-extent convention). 2–5k boxes total.
  3. ME — the mantis-style data pipeline (adapters → COCO + provenance tags → dedup → per-sequence split), then fine-tune: start from COCO weights, lr ÷10, ~40–60 epochs, MLflow-tracked, per-camera mAP eval. Includes building the provenance enforcement mantis only documented.
DataModel: YOLOX-S · trained on: your labeled footage (yours, GDPR-clean) · CrowdHuman only as prototype benchmark, never shipped
Done testPer-camera mAP@50 ≥ 0.85 on held-out clips; visibly fewer misses in your worst lighting
PHASE 8

Training round B — YOUR Re-ID

Self-bootstrapped

What: fine-tune OSNet on your own cameras using the self-bootstrapping trick — the P2 tracker manufactures the training data: every clean track = one identity photographed dozens of times, zero manual labels for same-camera pairs.

  1. ME — harvest crops from confident tracks (quality-gated), auto-build identities, triplet + ID loss fine-tune with random-erasing (the occlusion augmentation your house demands).
  2. YOU — confirm/reject the cross-camera candidate pairs the system proposes (~30 min).
  3. ME — re-tune the P5/P6 thresholds on the new embedder; measure cross-camera IDF1 on the P6 labeled walks before/after.
DataModel: OSNet · trained on: tracker-harvested crops from your cameras + your confirmations. Clean provenance end to end.
Done testLoop-walk success rises measurably (e.g. 8/10 → 9-10/10); look-alike failures reduced and documented

Why this order and no other

Each phase's output is the next phase's input — the dependency chain is the argument.

Without……you cannot
P0 simulatortune P6's fusion engine against known ground truth (real ground truth costs a labeling day per clip)
P1 detectionstrack anything — no tracker can recover a person never detected
P2 stable tracksbuild Re-ID templates (P5) or harvest Re-ID training data (P8) — the tracklet is the atomic unit
P3 calibrationfuse anything geometrically (P4) or gate by space-time (P6) — no shared map, no physics
P4 multi-camera trackslearn the floor plan from footfall (P4A) — one camera's paths aren't enough to map a building
P5 appearancebridge blind gaps (P6), OR carry a face-label across cameras (P5A) — geometry can't see across them
P5A named identitylabel a dot "the client" — the anonymous tracker (P2/P6) must exist first to attach a name to
P6 working end-to-endknow what P7/P8 training should fix — you train against measured failures, not vibes
